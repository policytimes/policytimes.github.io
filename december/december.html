Editor's Desk

The month of November saw the Indian Government take significant steps to enable the Country to play an important role in re-building global supply chains, whilst promoting Atmanirbharta. The Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme was extended to ten different sectors in addition to electronics good manufacturing, with the automotive sector being a key component. An inter-ministerial committee has been set up, to bolster the Capital Goods Sector. New labour codes have been introduced, to accelerate ease of doing business and the Ministry of Finance introduced a new stimulus package ( Atmanirbharta 3.0),  focused on job creation.

Ministry of Home Affairs 

Stringent focus on surveillance and containment of COVID-19 (Unlock 5.0 extended till 31.12.2020)

Relevant to all Mahindra Businesses 

November 25, 2020 

  • The main focus of the Guidelines is to consolidate the substantial gains that have been achieved against the spread of COVID-19 which is visible in the steady decline in number of active cases in the country. 

  • Further, keeping in view the recent spike in new cases in few States/ UTs, ongoing festival season and onset of winter, it is emphasised that to fully overcome the pandemic, there is need to maintain caution and to strictly follow the prescribed containment strategy, focussed on surveillance, containment and strict observance of the guidelines/ SOPs issued by MHA and Ministry of Health & Family Welfare (MOHFW).

  • Local district, police and municipal authorities shall be responsible to ensure that the prescribed Containment measures are strictly followed.States and UTs, based on their assessment of the situation, may impose local restrictions, with a view to contain the spread of COVID-19.

Reserve Bank of India

Entry of Corporate into banking spaces recommended by Internal Working Group 

Relevant to MMFSL 

November 20, 2020 

  • In a series of proposals, an Internal Working Group (IWG) of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has recommended the guarded entry of corporates into the banking space, conversion of big NBFCs into banks and hike in promoters’ stake to 26 per cent from 15 per cent.

  • The group also proposed a hike in minimum capital for new banks from Rs 500 crore to Rs 1,000 crore.

  • The panel, headed by RBI executive director PK Mohanty, was set up in June to review extant ownership guidelines and corporate structure for Indian private sector banks.

FASTags mandatory for four wheelers from 1 January 2021 

Relevant to M&M Auto 

November 9, 2020 

  • FASTags are set to become mandatory on all four-wheelers from January 1, 2021, as per a recent notification by the Ministry of Road Transport and Highway. 

  • Previously, mandatory for four-wheelers sold from December 2017 onwards, the latest notification from the MoRTH makes FASTags mandatory for all four-wheelers, regardless of the year of manufacture.

  • Valid FASTags to be made mandatory for third-party insurance from April 2021
